Nico André Schmid

I am a fourth year Ph.D. student in the program of Business Economics at Ghent University in Belgium, advised by Prof. Veronique Limère. Throughout my Ph.D., I had the great and enriching opportunity to visit Prof. Benoit Montreuil at the Georgia Institute of Technology (USA). I expect to graduate in summer 2020. Therefore, I am looking for challenging and interesting academic positions in the role of an assistant professor in the field of operations research and management sciences.

Research interests

My Ph.D. thesis is on the optimization of assembly systems with respect to balancing and feeding decisions. This involves a lot of optimization using mixed-integer programming techniques. Within my current projects, I am got using constraint and stochastic programming. Since the field of study is quite practical, I aim to collaborate with companies whenever possible. Within my master thesis, I investigated the complexity of a job-shop scheduling problem. Furthermore, I am interested in disassembly line balancing and production facility layout.
